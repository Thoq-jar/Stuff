    The early days of coffee

    • 9th Century Ethiopian origins Coffee is believed to have been discovered by an Ethiopian goat herder named Kaldi, who noticed his goats became energetic after eating coffee berries.
    • 15th Century Spread to the Arabian Peninsula Coffee was cultivated and traded in the Arabian Peninsula. The first coffee trees were cultivated in Yemen. The earliest credible evidence of coffee drinking comes from Sufi monasteries in Yemen.
    • 16th Century Coffee in the Middle East, Persia, and Turkey Coffee spread to Persia, Egypt, Syria, and Turkey. Coffee houses, known as qahveh khaneh, appeared in the Middle East, becoming centers for social interaction. And 16th Century Coffee in the Americas and Asia Coffee plants were smuggled out of the Middle East by Baba Budan and taken to India. The Dutch provided a significant influence by cultivating coffee in Ceylon (Sri Lanka) and later in Java (Indonesia).
    • 17th Century Introduction to Europe Coffee made its way to Europe through Venetian trade. The first European coffee house opened in Venice in 1645. Coffee houses became popular in England, France, Germany, and Holland. These were often called "penny universities" due to the stimulating conversation found there for the price of a cup of coffee.
    • How Coffee Changed people's needs: When coffee became popular, people started to replace coffee to a breakfast drink instead of drinking beer or wine. But those who drank coffee instead of alcohol became more alert and energized.

    How we got to where we are today

    • 18th Century Coffee production spreads Coffee plants were introduced to the Caribbean, Central, and South America by European colonists. Coffee became a significant colonial crop in Brazil, which would become the largest coffee producer in the world.
    • 19th Century Rise of coffee culture and commercialization Coffee culture expanded with the invention of the espresso machine by Angelo Moriondo in 1884. The development of canned coffee and the introduction of instant coffee by Nestl√© during World War II made coffee more accessible.
    • 20th Century Industrial advancements and global popularity Espresso culture grew in Ital y and spread worldwide. Establishment and rise of global coffee chains like Starbucks in the 1970s.
    • 21st Century Specialty coffee movement and sustainability The specialty coffee movement emphasized high-quality beans, artisanal roasting, and brewing methods. Sustainable coffee production practices gained importance, with certifications like Fair Trade and Rainforest Alliance becoming popular. This timeline showcases coffee's journey from its discovery in Ethiopia to becoming a beloved global beverage, continuously improving in quality and accessibility.
